## Why Neural Networks?

Neural networks started with the motivation of trying to build software to mimic the brain. Work began back in the 1950s, gained popularity in the 1980s and 1990s with applications like handwritten digit recognition, then fell out of favor in the late 1990s.

Around 2005, neural networks re-emerged, rebranded as **deep learning**. This took off in the last decade and a half, with major breakthroughs in:

- **Speech recognition** - First major breakthrough area for modern deep learning
- **Computer vision** - The ImageNet moment in 2012 created widespread impact
- **Natural language processing** - Transforming how we process text and language
- **Many other applications** - Climate change, medical imaging, online advertising, product recommendations, and more

<Note>
Neural networks are now used in everything from climate change research to medical imaging, online advertising, and product recommendations.
</Note>
